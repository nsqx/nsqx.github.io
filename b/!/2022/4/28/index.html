Utilizing “Justice” by Langston Hughes, examine for tone in order to discover the speaker's feelings about the subject. Consider the use of voice, diction, imagery, figures of speech, symbolism and allegory, syntax, sound, rhythm and meter, and structure.

Poem - Justice

That Justice is a blind goddess

Is a thing to which we black are wise:

Her bandage hides two festering sores

That once perhaps were eyes

- Langston Hughes (1901 - 1967)
PEEL Response

In the poem Justice by famed activist, novelist, and poet Langston Hughes, the author writes about the how the American Justice system is unfair to African-Americans and how the African-Americans are aware of it. This is evident in the first two lines, where justice is compared to a "blind goddess" - a benevolent thing, but only to some - and this is known to the African-Americans. In the last two lines, Langston writes that justice - the blind goddess - used to see. This is a metaphor, a symbol, which conveys that in the past, justice was different, and saw for all - not just the privileged whites, but the blacks as well. Langston utilizes the symbol of the blind goddess to illustrate the past and present justice system. This poem connects with the African-American audience, and also protests against the unfairness of the justice system applied to African-Americans, a message targeted to the privileged whites.
